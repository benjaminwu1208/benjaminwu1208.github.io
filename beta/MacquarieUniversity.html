Service Connect Onboarding Optimisation

Read Case Study

Overview

The project client is Service Connect at Macquarie University.

Service Connect is Macquarie University's new Shared Service Centre. Its strategic objective is to provide a better and more consistent customer experience for students and staff while driving cost efficiencies through centralisation and simplification of University processes.

My role included developing research questions for Service Connect Staff to understand their challenges from day-to-day tasks, user interface design from low-fidelity to high-fidelity, user testing and client presentation.

Problem

Service Connect Staff at Macquarie University feels overwhelmed and confused when finding the right information for students. They want to be able to provide a better customer service experience but believe their onboarding and learning journey did not engage or prepare them for the job.

Solution

A gamified interactive platform for Service Connect Staff to engage with their onboarding and learning program, and able to better prepare for their daily routine to provide a better customer service experience and the right information for students.

Affinity Mapping
By combining the research findings from desktop research, competitor analysis, online survey, and interview, the affinity mapping organises vast amounts of qualitative data.

Onboarding & Induction
• Interaction and engagement lead to effective and positive learning
• Strong reliance on knowledge tools e.g. WorkDay modules, Connect Notebook
• An overload of information and poor navigation leads to a lack of engagement and knowledge retention

Initial Learning Journey
• It took staff a while to feel confident
• Buddy shifts were the most favoured for effective and efficient learning

Ongoing Learning Experiences
• Staff feel supported but become dependent on their managers for complex queries
• Staff feel overwhelmed by regular updates
• Confidence in the role was a result of repetitive practical experience

Tracking Learner Progress
• Casual staff do not have ways to formally track their progress unless managed by their team leader

Ongoing Training
• Many staff members want further training to be empowered with the right knowledge, confidence, and tools to answer complex queries for students
• 48% of Staff were not satisfied with the ongoing training and support

Onboarding Framework for Service Connect Staff

Mentorship Service Model

Concept
A new staff member begins their journey at Service Connect and is placed in a Mentorship Group consisting of a Team Leader/Senior Officer, experienced staff members, and other new recruits. They stay on the same mentorship team until the next recruitment cycle.

Features
• Support and feedback-driven from leadership and peers
• Learning is tracked as a group to view each team member's progression
• Accountability within the team to keep up with one another
• Check-ins as a team to encourage ongoing learning and team goal setting
• Friendly competition with other mentorship teams
• The natural progression for new trainees to eventually become mentors for the next recruitment cycle

Benefits
• Takes the well-loved buddy-shift to the next level and stretches it across the staff learning journey
• New and old staff have a support network to encourage each other
• Dependency on leadership and management reduced amongst staff members
• Draws upon MQ’s vision for staff to be accountable for their individual and team learning
• Staff members cycle through the team and transition roles as they gain experience
• Builds a tight-knit community that feel like family

Limitations
• Mentor group members aren't always on shift together
• Hinges on individual willingness to reach out and help one another
• Subject to changes in staffing

My Learnings

The importance of research
When our team was debriefed by the project, non of the team has a clue of what would be the outcome nor how a student service center works. Through research, we have narrowed down the project direction and were able to identify the pain points and design a solution around it.

Using the right tone
It is my first time working with a big team of people coming from different backgrounds, cultures, and generations. It was not as challenging as I thought because every single one of the teammates are open to opinions, feelings, and constructive feedback. With that said, I have learned that in order to express myself clearly to others, it is best to adjust the tone and wording based on whom I am talking to so that both of us can understand each other better.
